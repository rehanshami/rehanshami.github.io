The project

During the Skinny migration, I was responsible for creating and publishing all webpages in AEM. The project focused on boosting the user experience by removing errors while maintaining the Skinny look and feel.

Duration: 4 months

Methods: Scoping content, wireframes, image editing, researching, and content rewriting.

Tools: Adobe AEM, Adobe Photoshop, and Figma.

Context

Due to various business decisions, it was decided that the Skinny website would be migrated to AEM from Silverstripe. The primary objective of this project was to migrate all pages into AEM and resolve content and design related errors. I was responsible for creating pages, writing content and publishing pages in AEM, while maintaining the Skinny brand look and feel.

I collaborated closely with a User Experience Designer and stakeholders from Skinny to achieve the final outcome.

Challenges, insights, and outcomes

This project utilized brand new components to create webpages. Initially, the components contained bugs and were not useable from a content authoring point of view. I provided regular and continuous feedback to React and AEM developers to resolve these concerns.

When the project was scoped, it was determined that 27 pages would need to be created. This was not correct, as subsequent scopings indicated that there were closer to 160 pages. I was under time pressure to create a lot of pages in a short timeframe. I created multiple templates within AEM that I was able to recycle and use throughout the majority of the pages. This helped me stay on track with the time constraint.

The project is currently going through final adjustments and testing. It is scheduled to go live on the 4th of February 2021.
